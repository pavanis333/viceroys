# Viceroys of India - UPSC Prep App

Comprehensive chronological study app covering 9 Viceroys of India from 1905-1947 for UPSC CSE Modern India preparation.

## 9 Viceroys Covered (1905-1947)

1. **Lord Minto II** (1905-1910) - Muslim League, Morley-Minto Reforms
2. **Lord Hardinge II** (1910-1916) - Capital to Delhi, Delhi Darbar
3. **Lord Chelmsford** (1916-1921) - Jallianwala Bagh, Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms
4. **Lord Reading** (1921-1926) - Chauri Chaura, Moplah Rebellion
5. **Lord Irwin** (1926-1931) - Simon Commission, Civil Disobedience, 1st RTC
6. **Lord Willingdon** (1931-1936) - 2nd & 3rd RTC, Poona Pact
7. **Lord Linlithgow** (1936-1943) - Longest-serving, Quit India, WW2
8. **Lord Wavell** (1943-1947) - INA Trials, Cabinet Mission, Direct Action Day
9. **Lord Mountbatten** (1947) - Last Viceroy, Partition, Independence

## Key Coverage

### Major Events
- Foundation of Muslim League (1906)
- Transfer of Capital to Delhi (1911)
- Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (1919)
- Simon Commission (1927)
- Civil Disobedience (1930)
- Three Round Table Conferences (1930-32)
- Quit India Movement (1942)
- Partition & Independence (1947)

### Key Reforms
- Morley-Minto Reforms (1909) - Separate electorates
- Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919) - Dyarchy
- Communal Award (1932)

### Special Facts
- Lord Linlithgow: Longest-serving Viceroy
- Lord Mountbatten: Last Viceroy
- Declared war without consulting Congress (1939)
